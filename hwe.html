Hardy-Weinberg Equilibrium

Combining alleles into genotypes according to their frequency.

Allele Frequency: Choosing Variants at Random.

If we imagine a population with only two alleles A and a, we can estimate the abundance of each by calculating its frequency. The frequency of allele A (denoted with the letter p) is the fraction of all alleles in the population which are A. So if we imagine all the sperm and eggs made by all the individuals in a population, then p represents the chance of choosing one of these gametes at random and finding that it contains allele A.

When there are only two alleles in the population, the gamete we've chosen will always contain either A or a. So the frequency of allele a is the chance that we didn't find allele A, denoted with the letter q or equivalently (1 − p).

Equation 1: If there are two alleles, then the probability of a randomly chosen gamete containing either allele A (p = freq(A)) or allele a (q = freq(a)) is 1.

(p + q) = 1

Genotype Frequency: Choosing Individuals at Random.

Diploid individuals result from the fusion of two haploid gametes. The Hardy-Weinberg Equilibrium equation tells us how many of each genotype to expect when we randomly choose two alleles and combine them. If we know the allele frequencies (p and q) then we can use the Hardy-Weinberg Equilibrium equation to predict the genotype frequencies — how many individuals will be AA, or Aa, or aa.

We are imagining creating diploid individuals by sampling two gametes from the population at random. The chance of getting genotype AA depends on the frequency of the A allele — it is the chance of drawing an A allele twice in a row: this is p AND p again, which is the same as p². So by the same logic, the chance of sampling an aa genotype is q², the frequency of the a allele squared. Since there are two ways to draw the alleles that make a heterozygote (either Aa or aA) the probability of sampling a heterozygote is 2pq.

Equation 2: Because we sample two alleles to make each genotype, the expected frequency of each genotype depends on the frequency of each allele.

(p + q)² = p² + 2pq + q² = 1
